About

The story behind UniCalc

We got tired of Googling merit formulas and getting wrong answers. So we built the thing we wished existed.

If you've ever applied to a Pakistani university, you know the drill. Every university has its own merit formula. Some weight your Matric at 10%, others at 40%. Some convert A-Level grades differently. And good luck finding the actual formula. You'll get five different answers from five different sources.

UniCalc started because of that exact frustration. Instead of sharing screenshots of Excel sheets in WhatsApp groups, we thought: why not just build one place that does it all, correctly?

So that's what this is. A single calculator that knows the official merit formula for every major university in Pakistan. You punch in your marks, pick your university, and get your aggregate. No guesswork, no outdated PDFs, no "bhai ye formula sahi hai?" in group chats.

We update the formulas every admission cycle. The merit data comes from verified sources and is cross-checked by contributors who've actually been through the process. And the whole thing is free. No sign-ups, no paywalls, nothing.

UniCalc is also Pakistan's first open-source university merit calculator. The entire codebase is public. Anyone can inspect, contribute, or suggest improvements. We believe transparency builds trust, especially when students are making decisions that shape their future.
